Quiz Questions

1. Briefly define what is meant by a “survey of the New Testament.”

2. True or False: The New Testament was originally written primarily in Koine Greek.

3. List the four Gospels in order.

4. Which of the following best describes the purpose of the four Gospels?

5. Fill in the blank: The book of ________ serves as a bridge between the Gospels and the Epistles, showing the birth and expansion of the Apostolic Church.

6. True or False: The book of Acts is essential for understanding the Apostolic pattern of salvation and the operation of the Holy Ghost.

7. Briefly describe the main theme of the book of Acts.

8. Which of the following statements about the Epistles is most accurate?

9. Name at least two major themes commonly found in Paul’s Epistles.

10. True or False: Romans is especially important for understanding justification by faith, the righteousness of God, and the gospel to Jew and Gentile.

11. Briefly explain the unique role of the book of Hebrews in connecting the Old and New Testaments.

12. Which of the following best describes the “General Epistles” (James, 1–2 Peter, 1–3 John, Jude)?

13. In a sentence or two, describe the primary focus of 1 Corinthians.

14. True or False: The Gospels present the words and works of Jesus, while the Epistles explain the meaning and application of His work to the Church.

15. Fill in the blank: The book of ________ is the primary New Testament book of prophecy, filled with visions concerning the end times.

16. Which of the following is a good reason for Apostolic ministers to study a survey of the New Testament?

17. Give one example of how misunderstanding the New Testament context can lead to a wrong doctrine or practice.

18. True or False: A proper New Testament survey will always keep the Book of Acts central for understanding the church’s origin, message, and mission.

19. Briefly mention how the New Testament reveals Jesus as the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecy.

20. In two or three sentences, explain why a solid survey of the New Testament is essential for Apostolic preaching, teaching, and evangelism.

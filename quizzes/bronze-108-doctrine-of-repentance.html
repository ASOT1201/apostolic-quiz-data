Quiz Questions

1. Define “repentance” using its basic biblical meaning (change of mind, heart, and direction).

2. True or False: Repentance is only feeling sorry for sin, without any change in behavior.

3. Fill in the blank: Jesus began His public ministry by saying, “Repent: for the ________ of heaven is at hand.”

4. Which of the following best describes “godly sorrow” according to 2 Corinthians 7:10?

5. Briefly describe at least two “fruits” or evidences of true repentance.

6. True or False: Repentance and faith toward God are both listed as foundational doctrines in Hebrews 6:1.

7. Fill in the blank: John the Baptist preached, “Bring forth therefore fruits meet for ________.”

8. Which statement best reflects Apostolic teaching on repentance?

9. Explain how repentance relates to the Old Testament prophets’ call to return to the LORD.

10. True or False: A person can genuinely repent while still planning to continue in known sin.

11. Briefly explain how repentance prepares the heart for water baptism in the name of Jesus.

12. Which of the following best describes the relationship between repentance and forgiveness?

13. Give one example from the New Testament (book and reference) where repentance is clearly preached.

14. True or False: Repentance is a one-time act and never again needed in the believer’s life.

15. Fill in the blank: In Revelation, Jesus calls the churches to “remember therefore from whence thou art fallen, and ________, and do the first works.”

16. Describe how a proper understanding of repentance affects counseling, altar work, and evangelism in Apostolic ministry.

17. Which is a sign of worldly sorrow rather than godly repentance?

18. True or False: Repentance includes both turning from sin and turning to God.

19. Briefly explain what it means to “repent and believe the gospel” (Mark 1:15).

20. In two or three sentences, summarize why the doctrine of repentance is vital for every Apostolic believer and minister.

Quiz Questions

1. In your own words, define “biblical hermeneutics.”

2. True or False: A literal interpretation means we ignore figures of speech and read every verse in a wooden, rigid way.

3. Fill in the blanks: Sound interpretation must consider the _______ setting and the _______ setting of a passage.

4. Which term best describes interpreting a verse in light of the verses before and after it?

5. Give one example of a passage that uses figurative language (such as metaphor, simile, personification, or symbolism) and briefly describe the figure of speech.

6. True or False: The same verse can never have both a literal sense and an application that is spiritual or symbolic.

7. Fill in the blank: A _______ is a comparison using “like” or “as,” while a _______ is a direct comparison without “like” or “as.”

8. Which of the following best describes “exegesis”?

9. What is “eisegesis,” and why is it dangerous in biblical interpretation?

10. True or False: A text of Scripture can never mean what it never meant to the original audience.

11. List at least three key questions a Bible student should ask when interpreting a passage (for example: Who? What? When? Where? Why? How?).

12. Which option best describes “historical-grammatical” interpretation?

13. Give a brief example of how ignoring historical background can lead to a wrong interpretation of a verse or passage.

14. True or False: Parables should normally be interpreted by focusing on the main point, not by giving every detail its own mystical meaning.

15. Fill in the blank: “Scripture interprets ________.” Explain what this principle means.

16. Which of the following is the best approach when encountering difficult or unclear passages?

17. Describe the danger of building doctrine on a single verse taken out of its broader context.

18. True or False: Right interpretation must always lead to right application; we are responsible not only to understand but also to obey the Word.

19. Explain the difference between “descriptive” passages (that describe what happened) and “prescriptive” passages (that command what should happen), and why this matters in hermeneutics.

20. In two or three sentences, summarize why sound principles of biblical interpretation are especially important for Apostolic, Oneness ministers and teachers.

Quiz Questions

1. List the six “principles of the doctrine of Christ” found in Hebrews 6:1–2.

2. True or False: The foundations of faith are optional teachings that believers can ignore if they wish.

3. Fill in the blank: Hebrews 6:1 says, “Therefore leaving the ________ of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection.”

4. Which of the following is not listed in Hebrews 6:1–2?

5. Briefly explain why “repentance from dead works” must come before true faith toward God.

6. True or False: “Faith toward God” means trusting in God’s Word, character, and promises, resulting in obedience.

7. Fill in the blank: The “doctrine of baptisms” includes water baptism in Jesus’ name, Spirit baptism, and the baptism of ________.

8. Which statement best describes the significance of “laying on of hands”?

9. Explain how the “resurrection of the dead” is foundational to Christian hope and holy living.

10. True or False: “Eternal judgment” reminds the believer that our choices in this life have eternal consequences.

11. In your own words, describe the danger of trying to build a Christian life without these foundations firmly in place.

12. Which of the following best summarizes the purpose of foundational doctrine?

13. Briefly explain how repentance, faith, and baptism work together as part of the foundation of a believer’s life.

14. True or False: Once the foundations are laid, a believer never needs to revisit or review them again.

15. Give one scripture (book and reference) that supports the importance of faith for salvation.

16. Fill in the blank: Without a proper foundation, a building will eventually ________; likewise, a believer without sound doctrine will not be able to stand.

17. Which of the following is a practical way for believers to strengthen their foundations?

18. Describe how understanding eternal judgment can affect a minister’s preaching and a believer’s daily choices.

19. True or False: The goal of laying the foundation is that we might “go on unto perfection” (spiritual maturity).

20. In two or three sentences, summarize why the foundations of faith in Hebrews 6:1–2 are essential for Apostolic believers and ministers.
